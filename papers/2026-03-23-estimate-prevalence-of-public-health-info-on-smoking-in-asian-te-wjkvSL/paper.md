## Scope and interpretation

The available evidence does **not** directly measure the prevalence of public health information about smoking among Asian teens. Instead, it provides regional tobacco-use prevalence figures and a description of surveillance infrastructure.

## Evidence summary

- WHO reporting is consistent with the **WHO South-East Asia Region** having about **11 million** adolescent tobacco users aged **13-15 years**, or about **30% of the global total**, based on surveys conducted during **2012-2022** and applied to **2021** populations.
- The same WHO evidence is consistent with an average current tobacco-use prevalence of around **11%** among adolescents aged **13-15 years** in the **WHO South-East Asia Region**.
- Older CDC/WHO GYTS reporting suggests that, in **South-East Asia**, **any tobacco use** among students aged **13-15 years** was about **12.9%** and **cigarette smoking** was about **4.3%** in surveys conducted during **1999-2005**.
- CDC describes **GTSSData** as publicly accessible surveillance infrastructure for youth tobacco, covering **more than 20 tobacco-data topics** across **four survey families**.

## Limitations

- These figures are **regional**, especially for the **WHO South-East Asia Region**, and should not be generalized to all of Asia without additional evidence.
- The sources primarily measure **tobacco-use prevalence** and **survey coverage**, not exposure to or prevalence of public health information itself.
- Some records are based on authoritative web snippets and landing pages rather than raw microdata.
- The figures combine surveys from different years and methods, so direct comparison should be cautious.
- This is a **conservative extraction ledger**, not a full systematic review.

## Source basis

The evidence is drawn from WHO and CDC sources associated with the South-East Asia Region and CDC's GTSSData portal.
