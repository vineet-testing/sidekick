# Evidence gap memo

## Scope
This memo summarizes the retrieved public pediatric responsive neurostimulation (RNS) literature and focuses on whether sex-specific outcomes were available in the accessible text.

## What the retrieved evidence shows
- In one retrieved multicenter pediatric RNS cohort, 35 patients were included and 34% were female (12 patients).
- Among the retrieved pediatric RNS studies summarized here, sex-specific seizure outcomes or formal sex-difference analyses were not clearly reported in the accessible extracted text.
- The currently retrieved public evidence is sufficient to describe pediatric RNS cohort size and overall outcomes more confidently than to support claims about sex differences among pediatric RNS patients.

## Interpretation
Based on the saved evidence artifacts, the public pediatric RNS literature identified here supports a narrow statement about baseline sex representation in one cohort, but it does not support a stronger conclusion about sex differences in seizure outcomes.

## Limitations
- The retrieved pediatric RNS studies were small retrospective series or pooled literature analyses.
- Most retrieved abstracts did not provide sex-stratified outcomes.
- Only one retrieved full-text pediatric multicenter study clearly exposed baseline female representation in the accessible text.
- No strong claim about sex differences in seizure outcomes, complications, or quality of life among pediatric RNS patients was supportable from the saved evidence artifacts alone.
- Some relevant full texts may exist behind publisher access barriers and were not reproducibly extracted here.
